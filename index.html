About Myself

I grew up in Deerfield, Illinois and went to college in Nashville. I spent the greater part of my 20s in Washington D.C., then eventually landed New York City, where I attended urban planning graduate school.

Throughout my career, I have had the privilege of working on several critical social issues. At Arnold Ventures, a national strategic philanthropy, I conducted policy analysis and research to support a multi-million dollar grant portfolio aimed at criminal justice reform. At Washington D.C.'s Department of Behavioral Health, I managed federal resources to support community-based responses to the city's opioid overdose epidemic. These experiences - along with other roles like advocating for climate change solutions at ecoAmerica, mentoring young students at Higher Achievement, and tutoring in reading and writing at New York City public schools - have allowed me to enact a commitment to public service that guides me both personally and professionally. I aim to continue this ethic as I expand my career as an urban planner.

The urban environment coheres many issues that motivate my study and practice. I think it is critical that we address the acute crises of homelessness and housing insecurity while pursuing larger visions of decommodified housing. I am inspired by economic democracy movements that seek to create shared, community-based food and energy assets. I am interested in how alternative governance and profit-sharing structures can support local economic development. I am guided by the tenets of community-based participatory action research. If any of these topics resonate with you too, check out my reading list to see what is informing me at the moment and let's get in touch to discuss further!

Work Experience

I have nearly a decade of experience across government, consulting, non-profit, and philanthropic spaces advancing equitable community development through direct service, strategic grantmaking, research, and policy change.

As a graduate analyst at Karp Strategies, an NYC-based urban planning consulting firm, I...

  • Designed and conducted economic impact analyses and workforce development research to produce actionable insights into the feasibility of community development interventions, including land use changes and infrastructure investments
  • Conducted in-depth analysis of demographic, economic, and social trends and public policy to produce place-specific assessments and support the development of economic opportunity plans
  • Designed public outreach materials and planned community engagement events to inform the creation of town-wide community development strategies

As a State Opioid Response Project Monitor at Washington D.C.'s Department of Behavioral Health, I...

  • Monitored a portfolio of 17 grants totaling over $6MM of investments in community treatment services to improve behavioral health provider capacity and responsiveness to community members with opioid use disorder
  • Designed and implemented the federal State Opioid Response grant’s largest initiative to bolster DC’s ability to care for individuals with complex co-occurring physical and behavioral health conditions
  • Coordinated with multiple city agencies and community-based organizations to ensure cross-sector alignment and execute continual quality improvement
  • Implemented behavioral health research and programmatic initiatives to advance four strategic aims of the District-wide plan to end the city’s opioid overdose epidemic

As a Criminal Justice Analyst at Arnold Ventures, a national strategic philanthropy, I...

  • Conducted extensive research to identify and present opportunities for strategic investments to implement and sustain system improvements in the fields of policing, government data, mental illness, and housing instability
  • Managed a portfolio of grants totaling over $1MM of strategic investments to improve coordination among government actors to increase vulnerable populations’ access to and engagement with community-based services
  • Created high-impact reports and data visualizations to synthesize and present complex information to key audiences, including foundation co-founders, the foundation board, policymakers, and elected officials
  • Developed, instituted, and oversaw grant development and management processes to increase the efficiency and collaboration of the Arnold Ventures policing team, leading to a portfolio of 73 grants totaling over $60MM
